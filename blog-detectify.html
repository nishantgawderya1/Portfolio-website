Rooftop Detection — Detectify (1st Place)

Published on May 27, 2026 · 4 min read · by Nishant Gawderya
Computer Vision YOLOv8m Augmentation Competition

Detectify was a competition entry focused on rooftop detection from satellite imagery. The task required robust localization of rooftop structures under varied lighting, occlusion, and scale. Using a YOLOv8m backbone and custom augmentation tailored to aerial perspectives, the model outperformed competitors and secured 1st place.

Approach

Key contributions included domain-specific augmentation (tilt, simulated atmospheric effects, multi-scale cropping), careful tile-based inference for large images, and post-processing to merge overlapping detections. The training pipeline emphasized balanced class sampling and IoU-aware augmentation policies.

Results

The model achieved top ranking in the competition with consistent precision across diverse test tiles. The approach generalizes well to similar overhead detection tasks and can be extended to other infrastructure mapping problems.

Takeaways

  • Custom augmentation matching the data distribution is critical for overhead imagery.
  • Tile-based inference with context-aware merging reduces false positives at tile edges.
  • Practical deployment benefits from lightweight backbones and ONNX exports for edge inference.
