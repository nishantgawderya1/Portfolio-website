Weld AI — Radiographic Weld Defect Detection

Published on May 27, 2026 · 6 min read · by Nishant Gawderya
Computer Vision PyTorch MLflow Industrial AI

Weld AI is an end-to-end Automated Defect Recognition (ADR) system designed for radiographic weld inspection using deep learning. Trained on the RIAWELC dataset of 24,000 industrial X-ray images across 4 defect classes, the system combines a YOLOv8 detector with a U-Net segmentation head to both localize and precisely mask defects.

Why this matters

Manual radiographic inspection is time-consuming and requires certified inspectors. Automating this process reduces human error, increases throughput, and enables scalable quality assurance across manufacturing sites.

Pipeline

  • 1. X-ray Upload — raw radiographic image input
  • 2. Preprocessing — noise reduction, contrast normalization, resizing
  • 3. YOLOv8 Detection — bounding box localization of defect regions
  • 4. U-Net Segmentation — pixel-level mask generation for detected defects
  • 5. Pixel-to-mm Calibration — convert pixel measurements to real-world dimensions
  • 6. ASME Report Generation — structured PDF inspection report output

Results

We achieved a YOLOv8 baseline with mAP@0.5 = 0.87 and an F1 score of 0.93. Inference time is under 1.8 seconds on the evaluation hardware. The dataset has been versioned with DVC and training tracked via MLflow.

Next steps

Work continues on per-defect U-Net segmentation refinement, FastAPI serving for batch and real-time inference, ONNX export for edge deployment, and automated ASME-compliant PDF report generation.

Tech stack

YOLOv8 (Ultralytics), U-Net (PyTorch), MLflow, Docker, DVC, FastAPI, ONNX.
